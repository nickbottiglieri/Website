SightGain

Software Developer Internship, Summer 2019 & 2020
Tysons, VA

As a sophomore in college, SightGain really took a chance on me. I was still learning the foundational computer science principles and had a chance to work for the first time on a full stack web application.

I learned NodeJS, Express, React, JavaScript, HTML, CSS and the basics of how a web application works before I became a Junior in college.

When I came back after Junior year, I was truly ready to step up and make a difference on the team. During my Junior year of college & during this internship I felt like a real developer for the first time. I felt like I understood and could work on any task from the database to the html.

Working at a face paced startup was extemely exciting, waking up every morning with the opportunity to solve a new problem. Sometimes I had no idea what the day had in store for me, which can be incredibly fun. I traveled onsite to Andrew's Air Force Base to gather requirements, watching how the end user interacted with the app we had been creating, and noting improvements to make. SightGain was a perfect introduction to the entire software developement process from the ground up and I'm thankful for my small team of knowledgeable mentors.

University of Maryland

B.S. in Computer Science, earned May 2021
College Park, MD

My 4 years at Maryland was filled with fantastic and challenging professors, pushing me to improve in nearly every computer science class.

However, the students - my friends - are the ones who I think taught me the most. Collaborating on homeworks, projects, exams (just kidding) enabled me to fully comprehend the difficult problems, algorithms, and computer science ideas that we learned. I remember group sessions lasting all day - 12 hours or more sometimes - to finish implementing an EM algorithm for our 422 (machine learning) class. or a convolutional neural network in 426 (computer vision) that can identify people at a crosswalk. These are the kind of projects that kept me motivated and eager to pursue greater things.

Maryland gifted me with a love for learning and taught me what I love to do. I will always be grateful.
